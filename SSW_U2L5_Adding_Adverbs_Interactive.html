Adverbs Are Flexible

Unlike adjectives (which go before nouns), adverbs can move to different positions in a sentence.

Same Adverb, Three Positions

Carefully, she opened the door.
She carefully opened the door.
She opened the door carefully.

All three are correct. Choose the position that sounds best.

After the verb
She ran quickly.
Best for HOW adverbs
Beginning of sentence
Yesterday, we went to the park.
Best for WHEN adverbs
End of sentence
The children played outside.
Best for WHERE adverbs
Before the verb
She always helps her friends.
Best for frequency adverbs

What Did We Learn?

Click each card to reveal the answer.

What do adverbs describe?
Adverbs describe verbs (actions). They answer: HOW? WHEN? WHERE? TO WHAT EXTENT?
What makes adverbs special compared to adjectives?
Adverbs are flexible. They can move to different positions in a sentence. Adjectives stay before the noun.
Name the four common adverb positions.
1. After the verb
2. Beginning of sentence
3. End of sentence
4. Before the verb
What adverbs should you avoid in writing?
Weak, vague adverbs like really, very, a lot, so much, kind of, sort of. Use specific adverbs instead.
How do you add an adverb to a sentence?
1. Find the verb
2. Ask HOW? WHEN? WHERE?
3. Choose a specific adverb
4. Try different positions
5. Pick the one that sounds best
