Using Multiple Describing Words

Combining Adjectives and Adverbs Effectively

Today's Goal: I can use multiple adjectives AND adverbs together to create detailed sentences WITHOUT overloading them.

Key ideas for today:

• Combine adjectives + adverbs in the same sentence

• Use 2–3 adjectives per noun, 1 adverb per verb

• Use commas between adjectives (the "and" test)

• Quality over quantity — do not overload

Combining Adjectives and Adverbs

Step-by-Step Expansion

Base: The dog ran.

+ Adjectives: The small, brown dog ran.

+ Adverb: The small, brown dog ran quickly.

2 adjectives (noun) + 1 adverb (verb) = balanced.

COMMA RULE

Use commas between adjectives when you can say "and" between them.
"tall, green tree" (tall AND green ✓)

AVOID OVERLOADING

2–3 adjectives per noun, 1–2 adverbs per verb. Each word should add new information.

❌ Overloaded
The tiny, small, little, fluffy dog ran very, really fast.
✔ Balanced
The tiny, fluffy dog ran swiftly.

What Did We Learn?

Click each card to reveal the answer.

How many adjectives should you use per noun?
2–3 adjectives per noun, and each should add different information (e.g., size + colour, not big + huge + enormous).
When do you use commas between adjectives?
When you can put "and" between them and it sounds right.
"tall, green tree" ✓ (tall AND green)
"three small dogs" ✗ (three AND small?)
What does "overloading" a sentence mean?
Using too many describing words, especially ones that repeat the same idea. This makes sentences confusing instead of clear.
How do you combine adjectives AND adverbs?
Add adjectives before the nouns, and add adverbs near the verb.
"The small, brown dog ran quickly."
(2 adj + 1 adv = balanced)
What is the key rule for balance?
Quality over quantity. Choose the best 2–3 describing words rather than adding every word you can think of.
