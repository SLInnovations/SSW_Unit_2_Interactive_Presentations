Articles: A, An, The

Using the Right Article Before Nouns

Today's Goal: I can use the correct article (a, an, the) before nouns.

Key rules for today:

• Use A before consonant sounds

• Use AN before vowel sounds

• Use THE for specific things

• Listen to the SOUND, not just the letter

Quick Review: Multiple Describing Words

Which sentence is balanced (not overloaded)?

The big, huge, enormous, gigantic dog ran very really fast.
The large, fluffy dog ran swiftly.
The dog ran.
The tiny small little brown soft cute dog ran super very quickly.
Learn

The Three Articles

A
Before consonant sounds
a dog, a tree, a uniform
AN
Before vowel sounds
an apple, an egg, an hour
THE
For specific things
the sun, the book I read

Tricky Words: Listen to the SOUND

A unicorn — sounds like "yoo" (consonant sound)

AN hour — silent H, sounds like "our" (vowel sound)

AN honest person — silent H, sounds like "on-est" (vowel sound)

A university — sounds like "yoo" (consonant sound)

With adjectives: The article matches the sound of the next word. "An old house" (not "a old house") because "old" starts with a vowel sound.
