What is an Adverb?

Describing Words That Tell About Verbs

🎯 I can identify adverbs and understand that adverbs are words that DESCRIBE VERBS (actions).

Learn
Adverb

A word that describes a verb (action)

Hint: Many adverbs end in -ly

Learn

Quick Review: Adjectives vs. Adverbs

Adjectives

Describe NOUNS (things)

The big dog

Adverbs

Describe VERBS (actions)

The dog ran quickly

Quick Check

In "She sings beautifully," which word is the adverb?

"She" — it names who is singing
"sings" — it's the action
"beautifully" — it describes HOW she sings
Learn

Adverbs Answer FOUR Questions

H
How? (manner) — carefully, quickly, loudly, quietly, happily
W
When? (time) — yesterday, today, soon, later, always, never
W
Where? (place) — here, there, outside, inside, everywhere
E
To what extent? (degree) — very, completely, almost, quite

💡 Most common: HOW adverbs (many end in -ly)

Learn

The -ly Pattern

You can often turn an adjective INTO an adverb by adding -ly:

slow → slowly   |   quiet → quietly   |   careful → carefully

⚠️ But watch out!

Not ALL -ly words are adverbs: friendly, lovely = adjectives!

Not ALL adverbs end in -ly: fast, very, always, here

The -ly pattern is a CLUE, not a rule!

Learn

Quick Check ✓

"The children played outside yesterday." How many adverbs are in this sentence?

One — "outside" (WHERE)
Two — "outside" (WHERE) and "yesterday" (WHEN)
Three — "children," "outside," and "yesterday"
None — there are no -ly words
