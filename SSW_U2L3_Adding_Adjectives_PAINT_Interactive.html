Adding Adjectives to Paint a Picture

Using the PAINT A PICTURE Strategy

Quick Review: Adjectives

From last lesson:

✔ An adjective is a word that describes a noun

✔ Adjectives answer: What kind? How many? Which one?

✔ Adjectives usually come before the noun

Quick Check

In "The tall, old tree swayed," which words are adjectives?

"tall" and "old" — they describe the noun "tree"
"swayed" — it tells what the tree did
"tree" and "swayed"

🎨 PAINT A PICTURE Strategy

Use this strategy to add adjectives to your sentences:

P
Pick a noun in your sentence
A
Ask "What kind?"
I
Imagine what it looks, feels, sounds, smells, or tastes like
N
Name the describing word (adjective)
T
Test it — Does it make sense? Does it paint a clearer picture?
Learn

Use Your Five Senses! 🖐️

The best adjectives help readers experience what you're describing.

👁️
See
blue, tall, shiny, round, sparkling
👂
Hear
loud, quiet, squeaky, musical
🤚
Touch
soft, rough, smooth, bumpy
👃
Smell
sweet, stinky, fresh, smoky
👅
Taste
sour, sweet, salty, bitter

💡 When you IMAGINE (the "I" step), think about your senses!
