Quick Review: Complete Sentences

A complete sentence has:

✔ Subject (who or what)   ✔ Predicate (what they do)

✔ Capital at the beginning   ✔ Punctuation at the end

Example: The dog ran.

This is COMPLETE and CORRECT... but is it INTERESTING?

Quick Check

Is "The dog ran." a complete sentence?

Yes — it has a subject, predicate, capital, and punctuation
No — it is missing important parts
It's not a sentence because it's too short

Why Do Writers Expand Sentences?

🎨
Create Pictures
Help readers see, hear, and feel what you're describing
✨
Make It Interesting
Turn plain writing into something readers WANT to read
📖
Give Information
Details answer questions and help readers understand
🌟
Show, Don't Tell
Instead of "She was tired," show it with details!

Which would YOU rather read?

The girl walked.

The tired girl walked slowly home after a long day.

Learn

Basic Sentence vs. Expanded Sentence

Basic Sentence

Just subject + verb

Tells us the minimum

Correct but plain

The cat slept.

Expanded Sentence

Subject + verb + DETAILS

Tells us much more

Interesting and vivid!

The fluffy orange cat slept peacefully on the warm windowsill.
Learn

Six Magic Questions for Expanding Sentences

When you want to expand a sentence, ask:

W
WHO? — What kind of person, animal, or thing?
W
WHAT? — What exactly is happening?
W
WHERE? — What place? What location?
W
WHEN? — What time? What day?
W
WHY? — For what reason?
H
HOW? — In what way or manner?

💡 You don't have to answer ALL six — choose the most interesting details!
